# Abstract Writing Guide

## Goal

Write a bioinformatics abstract that a reviewer can parse in one pass:

1. clinical or biological problem,
2. study design and modalities,
3. main discovery or model,
4. validation,
5. implication.

## Preferred Abstract Logic

For bioinformatics papers, the most stable pattern is:

1. Unmet need in disease biology, therapy response, prognosis, or progression.
2. Study design with cohort and modality stack.
3. Main discovery, biomarker, niche, or model.
4. Key validation and outcome association.
5. Biological or translational implication.

## Structured Abstract Pattern

Use this when the journal prefers structured headings:

1. `Background` or `Purpose`: the unmet need.
2. `Methods` or `Experimental Design`: cohorts, assays, model-building, and validation setup.
3. `Results`: main findings with direction and support.
4. `Conclusions`: implication and caution level.

## Unstructured Abstract Pattern

When the journal wants one paragraph:

1. Sentence 1: disease context and unmet need.
2. Sentence 2: study design and modalities.
3. Sentence 3: main discovery or model.
4. Sentence 4: strongest validation or clinical association.
5. Sentence 5: implication.

## Writing Rules

1. Mention the modality stack only when each modality matters to the story.
2. Do not list every algorithm or package.
3. State the main finding with direction.
4. Distinguish correlation from intervention.
5. Do not claim clinical utility without clear validation support.

## Checklist

1. Can a reader identify the unmet need, data design, major finding, and implication in one pass?
2. Are all major claims supported by cohorts, assays, or validation?
3. Is the wording cautious enough for observational evidence?
4. Is the abstract centered on one main story rather than a list of analyses?
