# Conclusion Writing Guide

## Goal

Close the paper with clear biological takeaways and credible translational boundaries.

## Structure

1. Restate the clinical or biological problem.
2. Summarize the central discovery or model.
3. State the strongest evidence and validation level.
4. State biological or translational impact.
5. Add a limitation paragraph.
6. End with the next decisive dataset, assay, or experiment.

## Limitation Guidance

Prefer limitations tied to:

1. cohort composition,
2. sample size,
3. modality coverage,
4. missing external validation,
5. missing functional evidence,
6. causal inference boundaries.

Avoid framing the conclusion around minor implementation details unless they change scientific interpretation.

## Template

1. This study addressed [clinical or biological problem] using [cohort or modality design].
2. We identified [main discovery or model], supported by [validation evidence].
3. These findings suggest [biological or translational meaning].
4. A current limitation is [scope boundary], and [future dataset, assay, or experiment] is an important next step.
