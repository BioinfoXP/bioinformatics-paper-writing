# Related Work Writing Guide

## Goal

Position the work against the most relevant biological and computational literature, and make the study gap easy to verify.

## Workflow

1. List directly competing disease-area and methodology papers first.
2. Group literature by scientific question, not by publication year alone.
3. For each topic, summarize what the field already knows and what remains unresolved.
4. End each topic by clarifying what your study adds.

## Topic Design

Use 2-4 focused topics, for example:

1. Disease-specific biology or biomarker studies.
2. Modality-specific studies:
   - bulk transcriptomics,
   - single-cell,
   - spatial omics,
   - pathology AI,
   - proteomics.
3. Studies closest to your central discovery or model.

## Paragraph Template

1. Topic sentence: define the scientific theme.
2. Representative studies: one compact synthesis.
3. Limitation tied to your paper's unresolved gap.
4. Transition sentence that leads to your study.

## Do and Don't

1. Do compare biological scope, cohort design, and modality resolution.
2. Do emphasize the exact gap your study fills.
3. Do not make Related Work a citation dump.
4. Do not hide strongest or newest competing studies.

## Checklist

1. Are all strongest or most relevant studies covered?
2. Is each topic connected to your disease setting or biological question?
3. Is your difference explained in scientific terms, not marketing terms?
4. Is citation coverage complete for all core claims?
