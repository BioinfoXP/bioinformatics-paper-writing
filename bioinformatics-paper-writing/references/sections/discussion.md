# Discussion Writing Guide

## Goal

Turn results into a biological interpretation without overstating certainty.

## Discussion Structure

1. Restate the central discovery in one to two sentences.
2. Explain how the combined modalities support that discovery.
3. Connect the finding to prior biology or clinical context.
4. Clarify translational significance.
5. State limitations honestly.
6. End with the next decisive experiment, dataset, or clinical test.

## Language Guidance

Use layered certainty:

1. `suggests` for a hypothesis supported by correlative evidence,
2. `supports` when multiple orthogonal assays align,
3. `indicates` when the evidence is strong but still observational,
4. `demonstrates` only when the data truly establish the claim.

## Strong Discussion Moves

1. Explain why one modality alone would have been insufficient.
2. Explain how tissue architecture or cell-cell context changes interpretation.
3. Distinguish prognostic association from mechanistic causality.
4. Tie limitations to what evidence would be needed next.

## Weak Discussion Moves

1. Repeating Results without synthesis.
2. Claiming therapeutic actionability from a single retrospective association.
3. Hiding cohort bias or sample-size limitations.
4. Discussing every pathway equally instead of prioritizing the central axis.
